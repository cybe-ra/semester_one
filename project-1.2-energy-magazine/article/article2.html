Best Budget Laptop? Lenovo Legion Y520

Personal Review - 11 October 2017

Oh, Lenovo, a chinese company. Can you trust it? Well yeah, not sure about all chinese companies, but this one is totally one to trust.

Lenovo Legion 520Y

Is it hard to imagine a gaming laptop budget? Well it is, because we talk about an 1000$ price laptop, so, it may not sound of budget anymore. But the truth, it is a budget laptop with latest technologies on.

Today, we have the most expensive laptop at a price of 10.000$, followed by an 8000$ laptop, so yes, you can call this 1000$ Lenovo laptop a budget one, compared to the others two.

What does this laptop comes with for 1000$? Well depends on what configuration you want it, mine comes with an Intel i5-7300HQ quad-core CPU with a freqvency of 2,5Ghz, going up to 3,5Ghz, 8GB of DDR4 RAM, 1Tb HDD with 5400rpm, 128Gb SSD M.2 and a very strong and powerful nVidia 1050Ti with 4Gb of VRAM GDDR5 memory. In other words, these are the latest generations of components.

Design

I was somehow scared about it in pictures, because it looks somehow fragile and not so solid, but after you get to put your hand on it, you will definetly change that impression. It feels solid, the plastic on the back looks like a carbon fiber, but it is not. In a full black, without any other design on the oustide, it may look like a normal laptop, but when you open it, you see the beauty of a red light keyboard, two front speakers under the screen, somehow a glossy and not so nice pad.

Performance

Latest generation of CPU from Intel and GPU from nVidia are on this model, so you can expect for it to be able to handle almost anything you ask. From gaming (which was designed for), to video randering, photoshop, and many other things, you will not get to force this device to use 100% of it's CPU. You just can't.

Screen, Speaker, Camera and Microphone

The screen is nice, looks good, at least for me, does not reflect anything from background, so you can focus on, it has a wide angle, high luminosity level for the day, low level for the night. It gets like 50% colour accuracy on Adobe sRGB test, so it is not really for professional photos, but more likely for a free time hobby.

The speakers are quite loud, they are in front, under the screen, so you will always hear them, they don't distort the sound too much at a high volume, but they lack of bass, but for a laptop, there are very good speakers.

Camera and microphones are decent, a normal 720p camera and actually two microphones for a good quality during a real chat, you can use it without problems for a simple Skype call, or a online game voice-chat.

Battery

You should not expect to be able to play too much on this laptop away from the plug, because it has only a 45Wh battery, you may expect for a maximum of one hour of gaming, and up to 4 hours of office task. So, luckly, you may be able to see a movie without the plug, but that's it.

Conclustion

Does it worth 1000$? Well, yes, if you need a strong laptop, or a laptop you want to keep for at least 5 years, and not to buy another one every two years yes. For some people, this machine may have too much power and they will not be able to use all of it, but for a gamer, or an hardocre user, this laptop is one of the best. You have latest CPU and GPU generation, an SSD with an HDD, USB Type-C, a good screen, and backlight keyboard.
